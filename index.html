About

Proposal Overview

We propose 2nd DialDoc Workshop that focuses on document-grounded dialogue and conversational question answering. There is vast amount of document content created every day by human writers to communicate with human readers for sharing knowledge, ranging from encyclopedia to social benefits. Making the document content accessible to users via conversational systems and scaling it to various domains could be a meaningful yet challenging task. There are significant individual research threads that show promise in handling heterogeous knowledge embedded in documents for building conversational systems, including (1) unstructured content, such as text passages; (2) semi-structured content, such as tables or lists; (3) structured data specified by schema (e.g., RDFa) in the webpages, and so on. The purpose of proposing this workshop is to invite researchers and practitioners to bring their individual perspectives on the subject of document-grounded dialogue and conversational question answering to advance the field in a joint effort.

Topics of interests include but not limited to

  • document-grounded dialogue and conversational machine reading, such as CMUDoG, DREAM, ShARC and Doc2Dial;
  • open domain dialogue and conversational QA, such as ORConvQA, TopiOCQA, Abg-CoQA, QReCC and MultiDoc2Dial;
  • parsing semi-structured document content for dialogue and conversational QA, table reading, such as HybridQA and TAT-QA;
  • summarization for dialogue, query-based summarization, such as AQUAMUSE;
  • evaluations for document-grounded dialogue;
  • interpretability and faithfulness in dialogue modeling.

Special Theme

This workshop focuses on scaling up document-grounded dialogue systems especially for low-resource domains, e.g., the applications in low-resource languages and emerging unforseen situations such as Covid-19 pandemic. We seek submissions that tackles the challenge on different aspects, including but not limited to

  • effective adaption of pre-trained models;
  • data augmentation and data generation for dialogue models;
  • domain adaption and meta learning for knowledge-grounded dialogue;

We also maintain a list of resources on COVID-19 dataset in different languages. Please contact us if you would like to suggest a related dataset.

About

We propose a shared task on building open-book goal-oriented dialogue system, where an agent could provide an answer or ask follow-up question for clarification given documents from multiple domains. The main goal is to generate agent response in natural language as well as providing the corresponding grounding evidence based on the domain documents. The training data is from several low-resource domains related to social welfare and the test data includes one more unseen domain on COVID-19.

DialDoc Workshop would like to offer prizes to top participant teams.

Data and Tasks

The training data will include the training and validation split from MultiDoc2Dial. Since the data in the target domains are all low-resource, we enourage the teams to explore existing public datasets and generate data for augmentation. The test data will be derived from MultiDoc2Dial dataset including multiple seen domains and unseen COVID-19 domain.

There will be two subtasks. One is focused on identifying the grounding evidence of agent's next response, the other is focused on generating agent response in natural language.

Evaluations

The output of the models/systems will be evaluated based on both automatic metrics and human evaluations.
